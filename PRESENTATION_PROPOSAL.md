# Presentation-Ready Project Proposal

## PawSagip Connect
### A Community Animal Rescue, Case Management, and Adoption Coordination Platform

---

## 1. Project Overview

PawSagip Connect is a proposed digital platform that helps communities report, track, and respond to animal-related incidents in one system. It is designed to support lost pet recovery, found animal reporting, cruelty case documentation, rescue coordination, and adoption processing.

The project brings together two important sides of animal welfare work:

- a public-facing platform for citizens and pet owners
- an operations-facing platform for barangays, rescuers, and shelters

Instead of relying on scattered Facebook posts, private messages, and manual follow-ups, PawSagip Connect provides one structured environment for reporting, coordination, and case monitoring.

---

## 2. Background of the Problem

In many communities, animal-related incidents are still handled informally. When a pet goes missing, a stray is found, or cruelty is witnessed, people usually post on social media or contact local responders manually.

This creates several problems:

- reports are scattered and difficult to track
- important details such as location and evidence are often incomplete
- rescue actions are not consistently documented
- shelters and barangays do not share one organized case system
- adoption opportunities are not centralized
- citizens do not know whether their report is being acted on

Because of this, rescue response is slower, adoption processing is less organized, and accountability is weak.

---

## 3. Problem Statement

There is no centralized platform that allows citizens, barangays, rescuers, and shelters to manage pet-related incidents and animal welfare cases in a coordinated and trackable way.

As a result:

- lost pets are harder to recover
- found animals remain unidentified longer
- cruelty cases may be delayed or overlooked
- rescue coordination is inconsistent
- adoption processing is fragmented
- local stakeholders lack reliable data for monitoring and action

---

## 4. Proposed Solution

The proposed solution is PawSagip Connect, a community-based platform that centralizes:

- pet incident reporting
- live alert browsing
- search and case discovery
- rescue and case management
- adoption application processing
- user activity tracking

The platform is designed to create a complete workflow from public report submission to operational response and final case outcome.

---

## 5. General Objective

To develop an integrated community animal welfare platform that supports reporting, rescue coordination, case monitoring, and adoption management in a single system.

---

## 6. Specific Objectives

The project aims to:

- provide an accessible platform for reporting lost, found, and cruelty-related cases
- allow users to search and monitor ongoing animal-related incidents
- support barangays and rescue teams in reviewing and managing cases
- provide shelters with a structured adoption workflow
- allow users to track reports and adoption applications
- establish a foundation for future pet registry and QR-based identity features

---

## 7. Target Users

The proposed system will benefit:

- pet owners
- concerned citizens
- adopters
- barangay administrators
- rescue volunteers
- shelter personnel
- local government units

---

## 8. Key Features

### For Public Users

- submit lost, found, cruelty, and adoption-related reports
- upload photos or videos as supporting evidence
- view live alerts and browse animal-related cases
- search reports and adoptable pets
- apply to adopt available animals
- track personal reports and applications

### For Operational Users

- review and monitor submitted cases
- update case status and response progress
- manage adoption applications
- organize records by barangay or organization
- maintain better oversight of rescue and adoption workflows

---

## 9. Scope of the Project

The scope of the project includes:

- community reporting

- adoption application handling
- administrative case monitoring
- shelter and barangay coordination

- QR-based pet registry
- owner verification

## 13. Newly Added: Project Limitations

- the platform depends on internet availability and device access
- report quality depends on user honesty and completeness of submitted information
- location-based accuracy depends on user permissions and correct map input
- the system supports coordination, but does not guarantee actual rescue response
- adoption approval still depends on human review by shelters or administrators
- some future features such as registry and QR identity may remain partial in early deployment
