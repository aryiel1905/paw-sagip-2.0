# Title

**PawSagip: A Community-Based Pet Rescue and Adoption Platform for the Philippines**

---

# Background of the Study

Animal welfare remains a growing concern in many communities in the Philippines. Cases involving lost pets, found animals, stray animals, abandonment, and cruelty are frequently encountered, yet the systems used to report and respond to these concerns are often informal and fragmented. In most communities, citizens rely on Facebook posts, group chats, personal messages, barangay hotlines, or word of mouth to seek help. While these methods may spread information quickly for a short period, they do not provide a reliable and organized process for case monitoring, rescue coordination, or follow-up action.

This lack of a centralized system results in delayed response, scattered information, and weak coordination among stakeholders. Lost pets are not easily reunited with their owners because reports are posted in separate places and become difficult to trace. Found animals may remain unclaimed or be handled without proper guidance. Cruelty cases may not reach the proper authorities in time, which limits intervention and documentation. At the same time, citizens who want to adopt animals have no single platform where they can view available pets and complete a structured application process.

These issues reveal the need for a digital platform that can centralize reporting, improve visibility of cases, and support a more efficient response process. PawSagip is proposed to address this need by providing a web-based platform that connects citizens and barangay-level operations in one accessible system.

---

# Project Overview

PawSagip is a web-based community pet rescue and adoption platform designed to centralize animal welfare-related reporting and coordination. The platform enables users to report lost pets, found animals, and cruelty incidents, while also giving the public access to community alerts and adoption opportunities. Its goal is to provide a structured and reliable digital environment where pet-related cases can be documented, monitored, and acted upon more efficiently.

The project is organized around two major components. The first is the citizen-facing platform, which allows the public to submit reports, search for alerts, browse adoptable animals, and access emergency support information. The second is the administrative side of the platform, which supports barangay-level monitoring and case management. These components are connected through a shared real-time database, ensuring that information is updated promptly and can be used for better coordination and recordkeeping.

---

# Project Purpose and Objectives

The purpose of PawSagip is to provide a centralized and accessible digital platform that improves the reporting, coordination, and monitoring of pet rescue, adoption, and animal welfare concerns in Philippine communities. The project aims to replace scattered and inconsistent communication methods with a more organized, community-based system.

The specific objectives of the project are:

1. To provide citizens with an accessible platform for reporting lost pets, found animals, and cruelty cases.
2. To centralize pet-related and animal welfare information in one system for easier tracking and monitoring.
3. To improve coordination and response efficiency at the barangay level through structured case management.
4. To support pet adoption by providing a platform where available animals can be viewed and adoption applications can be submitted online.
5. To promote responsible pet ownership and stronger public participation in animal welfare activities.
6. To establish a scalable foundation for future pet registry and identification features.

---

# Description / Key Feature of the Study

The study focuses on the development of a web-based platform that integrates reporting, monitoring, and adoption functions into one system. PawSagip is designed to provide an easier and more efficient way for communities to respond to animal welfare concerns.

Its key features include:

- **Incident Reporting**
  Users can submit lost, found, and cruelty reports with descriptions, uploaded images, and location details.

- **Real-Time Alert Feed**
  The platform displays community alerts in real time, improving awareness of recent cases.

- **Search and Filtering**
  Users can search alerts and adoption listings by keyword, category, or location.

- **Adoption Center**
  Citizens can browse adoptable animals and review relevant details before applying.

- **Online Adoption Application**
  The system supports a guided application process for individuals interested in adoption.

- **Application Tracking**
  Users can monitor the status of their adoption applications.

- **Emergency Access**
  The platform provides access to hotline and location support for urgent cases.

- **Role-Based Administration**
  Authorized users can review and manage case records according to their assigned access level.

- **Future Registry Readiness**
  The system is designed to support future pet registry integration through QR code or microchip identification.

# Why the Project Stands Out

PawSagip stands out because it does not focus on a single animal welfare concern alone. Instead, it combines reporting, community alerts, case visibility, and adoption support within one platform. This makes the system more practical and responsive to the actual needs of communities.

The project is also distinctive because it is community-based and aligned with barangay-level operations. Rather than functioning as a general listing site, it is designed to support local coordination and localized response. Its mobile-friendly design, real-time features, and structured workflow make it more suitable for actual field use and day-to-day community interaction.

Another reason the project stands out is its potential for expansion. The platform is not limited to current reporting and adoption functions. It can serve as the foundation for future pet registry, case analytics, and broader local government integration.

---

# Benefits and Beneficiaries

PawSagip offers several benefits to different groups within the community.

**For citizens**, the platform provides a more convenient and reliable way to report cases, search for lost or found pets, and access adoption opportunities. It reduces dependence on scattered online posts and improves access to organized information.

**For pet owners**, the project improves the chances of reuniting with lost pets by making case reporting and public visibility more systematic and searchable.

**For barangay officials**, the system provides a centralized platform for monitoring incidents, documenting reports, and supporting better coordination of community animal welfare concerns.

**For communities**, PawSagip encourages awareness, participation, and responsibility toward animal welfare issues. It supports a more organized response system that can lead to faster action and better outcomes.

Overall, the project benefits both animals and people by improving communication, coordination, and accountability.

# Conclusion

PawSagip addresses a clear and relevant problem in many Philippine communities: the absence of a centralized and structured platform for pet rescue, case reporting, and adoption management. By combining incident reporting, real-time information access, and adoption support in one web-based system, the project offers a practical and technology-driven response to common animal welfare issues.

The project demonstrates that digital platforms can play an important role in improving coordination, strengthening public participation, and supporting barangay-level management of community concerns. As a result, PawSagip has strong potential to contribute to more responsive and organized animal welfare efforts.

---

# Recommendation

To strengthen the project further, the following recommendations are proposed:

1. Expand pilot implementation to selected barangays to gather actual user feedback and operational data.
2. Improve data validation and moderation processes to ensure report quality and reliability.
3. Add notification features such as SMS or email updates for reporters and applicants.
4. Complete future-ready functions such as QR-based or microchip-based pet identification.
5. Enhance analytics and reporting tools to support decision-making and local policy planning.
6. Promote adoption of the system through partnerships with local government units and community organizations.

These recommendations can help improve the usability, impact, and long-term sustainability of the project.
