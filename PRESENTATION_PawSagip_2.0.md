## Slide 2. Introduction to Animal Welfare Issues

Animal welfare remains a serious and recurring concern in many Philippine communities. Lost pets, stray animals, abandoned animals, and cruelty cases continue to affect both animals and the people who care for them.

These issues go beyond animal care alone. They also involve:

- Public safety
- Community responsibility
- Local government response
- Responsible pet ownership
- Shelter capacity and rescue coordination

In many cases, citizens are willing to help, but they do not have a reliable system for reporting incidents, finding accurate information, or connecting with the proper responders.

---

## Slide 3. Why the Issue Matters

When animal welfare concerns are not handled quickly and properly, several negative outcomes occur:

- Lost pets remain missing for long periods
- Found animals are not easily reunited with owners
- Stray and abandoned animals remain vulnerable
- Cruelty cases may go unreported or unresolved
- Shelters struggle to promote adoption effectively

This shows the need for a more organized, accessible, and technology-driven approach to community animal welfare management.

---

## Slide 4. Current Challenges

At present, most reporting and coordination are still done through informal channels such as:

- Facebook posts
- Group chats
- Barangay hotlines
- Personal messages
- Word of mouth

Although these channels can spread information, they are not structured systems. Reports can be buried quickly, duplicated, ignored, or lost over time.

As a result, rescue response becomes inconsistent and difficult to monitor.

---

## Slide 5. Specific Problems in the Current Setup

The current setup creates several operational problems:

- No centralized platform for lost, found, and cruelty reports
- No consistent way to assign cases to barangay responders
- No unified directory of adoptable animals from shelters
- No real-time tracking of rescue or adoption activities
- Limited visibility of ongoing cases within a barangay
- Weak documentation for reporting, monitoring, and decision-making

These limitations reduce efficiency, transparency, and coordination among citizens, barangay officials, and shelters.

---

## Slide 6. PawSagip as a Solution

PawSagip is proposed as a web-based community pet rescue and adoption platform that centralizes animal welfare reporting and coordination.

It is designed to connect:

- Citizens
- Pet owners
- Barangay administrators
- Rescue teams
- Animal shelters
- Local government units

The system provides one accessible platform where incidents can be reported, cases can be managed, and adoption opportunities can be promoted in an organized and timely manner.

---

## Slide 7. Project Overview

PawSagip is composed of two major components:

**1. Citizen-Facing Application**

- For reporting incidents
- For viewing alerts
- For searching pet-related cases
- For browsing adoptable animals
- For applying to adopt

**2. Administrative Dashboard**

- For reviewing submitted reports
- For assigning rescue actions
- For managing adoption applications
- For monitoring barangay-level operations
- For maintaining organized records

Both components are connected to a shared real-time database for faster and more reliable information flow.

---

## Slide 8. Project Goals

PawSagip aims to:

- Provide a reliable reporting platform for pet-related incidents
- Improve rescue coordination at the barangay level
- Support shelters through a structured adoption system
- Strengthen documentation and monitoring of animal welfare cases
- Promote responsible pet ownership and community participation
- Build a scalable foundation for future pet registry integration

In short, the project transforms scattered community efforts into a structured digital response system.

---

## Slide 9. Features and Functions: Citizen Side

The citizen-facing application includes the following core functions:

- Incident reporting for lost pets, found animals, and cruelty cases
- Photo and media upload for clearer documentation
- Map-based location pinning for more accurate case reporting
- Real-time community alert feed
- Search functionality for alerts and adoptable animals
- Adoption center for browsing available pets
- Guided online adoption application
- Application status tracking
- Emergency hotline and map navigation support

These features are designed to be mobile-friendly and easy to use in real-world situations.

---

## Slide 10. Features and Functions: Administrative Side

The administrative dashboard provides operational tools for authorized users such as barangay administrators, rescue teams, and shelter staff.

Key administrative functions include:

- Reviewing and validating incoming reports
- Assigning reports to rescue personnel
- Updating case status and response progress
- Managing shelter animal listings
- Processing and tracking adoption applications
- Managing users by role and scope
- Exporting data for documentation and compliance

This dashboard supports organized case management and local accountability.

---

## Slide 11. Feature Highlights

Some of the most important system highlights are:

- **Real-Time Alerts**
  Citizens and responders can view newly submitted reports as they appear.

- **Centralized Search**
  Users can search across alerts and adoption listings from one platform.

- **Adoption Workflow**
  Shelters contacted by baranggay can post adoptable pets and applicants can complete a guided process online.

- **Role-Based Access**
  Users only access the functions relevant to their responsibilities.

- **Pet Registry Readiness**
  The system is prepared for future QR code or microchip-based identification features.

---

## Slide 12. Technology Overview

PawSagip is built using modern web technologies suitable for a scalable and maintainable platform:

- **Frontend:** Next.js, React, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS
- **Backend:** Node.js API routes
- **Database:** Supabase / PostgreSQL
- **Authentication:** Role-based access with secure account management
- **Storage:** Cloud storage for uploaded media
- **Maps and Location:** Interactive maps and geocoding services
- **Deployment:** Vercel

This technology stack supports responsiveness, real-time updates, and future expansion.

## Slide 15. Long-Term Value of the Project

Beyond its immediate use, PawSagip creates a foundation for long-term improvements in animal welfare management.

Its long-term value includes:

- Scalable implementation across more barangays
- Future integration of QR-based or microchip-based pet identification
- Better data collection for planning and policy support
- Stronger collaboration between local government units and shelters
- Potential expansion into a wider national animal welfare support system

This makes the project not only useful in the present, but also sustainable for future development.

---

## Slide 16. Limitations

Despite its strengths, the project has several limitations that must be recognized:

- The platform depends on internet access and device availability
- Data accuracy depends on the quality of user-submitted reports
- Successful implementation requires active participation from barangays and shelters
- Some advanced features, such as full QR-based pet registry integration, are still in progress
- Video media processing and some monitoring enhancements are not yet fully complete
- Wider impact depends on adoption by communities and local authorities

These limitations indicate that the platform is a strong starting point, but continuous improvement and stakeholder support are necessary.

---

## Slide 17. Conclusion

PawSagip addresses a real and urgent problem in Philippine communities: the lack of a centralized and organized system for animal welfare reporting, rescue coordination, and pet adoption.

By combining citizen reporting, real-time alerts, adoption workflows, and barangay-level administration in one platform, PawSagip offers a practical digital solution that can improve response time, accountability, and community involvement.

The project demonstrates how technology can be used not only to solve operational problems, but also to promote compassion, responsibility, and coordinated action for animal welfare.
